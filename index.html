Stephen Hawking Tribute

A Brief History of Time

Stephen Hawking, a renowned theoretical physicist, was born on January 8, 1942, in Oxford, England. He was diagnosed with amyotrophic lateral sclerosis (ALS), also known as Lou Gehrig's disease, at the age of 21. Despite his disability, he made significant contributions to the field of physics.

Key Achievements:

  • 1965- Received his Ph.D. in theoretical physics from the University of Cambridge.
  • 1969- "Black Holes and Time Warps" was published.
  • 1974- "The Large Scale Structure of Space-Time" was published.
  • 1988- "A Brief History of Time" was published and became a best-seller.
  • 1998- "A Brief History of Time" was recorded in the Guinness Book of Records for being on The Sunday Times best-sellers list for 237 weeks.
  • 1999- Appeared on the The Simpsons.
  • 2001- Appeared on the documentary series The Real Stephen Hawking
  • 2002- Published "On The Shoulders of Giants".
  • 2003- Called the Invasion of Iraq a War Crime.
  • 2005- "Information Loss in Black Holes" and "Black Holes and the Information Paradox" were published.
  • 2006- He posted an open question on the internet "In a world that is in chaos politically, socially and environmentally, how can the human race sustain another 100 years
